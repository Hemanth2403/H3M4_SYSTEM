# Modern Trends in FinTech Cybersecurity: A Review of SIEM and Threat Intelligence (2023-2025)

**Abstract:** The rapid evolution of the financial technology (FinTech) sector has brought about significant advancements in digital banking and payment systems. However, this progress has also introduced a more complex and sophisticated cybersecurity threat landscape. This article provides a brief review of 5 recent research papers and industry reports (2023-2025) to identify the core findings and limitations of current cybersecurity defense strategies within the FinTech ecosystem, highlighting the shift from reactive monitoring to proactive, AI-driven collaborative intelligence.

---

### 1. Systematic Review of Cybersecurity Threats in FinTech (2023)
*   **Findings:** Systematic analysis reveals 11 primary cyber threats (including advanced phishing and MITM attacks) and 9 defense strategies. The research underscores that as FinTech becomes increasingly data-centric, 24/7 autonomous monitoring through enhanced Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) is no longer optional but a baseline requirement.
*   **Limitations:** Traditional static defense mechanisms are failing against AI-driven polymorphic malware. Most current solutions are "black boxes" that lack cross-sector transparency, making it difficult to stop widespread campaigns.

### 2. AI-Driven Security and Zero Trust Models in Banking (2024)
*   **Findings:** Incident reports from 2024 show a 53% surge in cyber-attacks within the global banking sector. The adoption of AI/ML for real-time data analysis identifies unusual transaction patterns significantly faster than rule-based systems. Zero Trust models (Never Trust, Always Verify) are becoming the gold standard for FinTech cloud infrastructure.
*   **Limitations:** The increasing sophistication of targeted attacks (specifically AI-powered ransomware-as-a-service) can sometimes bypass behavioral analysis if the initial breach is subtle enough.

### 3. Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) Framework (2025 Forecast)
*   **Findings:** The upcoming implementation of the DORA framework in January 2025 introduces stringent new mandates for ICT risk mitigation. Financial institutions are shifting towards unified risk management platforms that integrate third-party vendor safety with internal security metrics.
*   **Limitations:** The transition to these strict regulatory frameworks involves high operational overhead and requires significant technical debt repayment for legacy banking systems.

### 4. Strategic Shift: The State of Threat Intelligence Report (2025)
*   **Findings:** Threat intelligence has transitioned from a niche technical tool to a strategic business asset. 91% of security leaders now use threat intelligence to guide business investments. There is a clear trend towards "Strategic Threat Intel" that bridges the gap between technical indicators (IOCs) and business risk.
*   **Limitations:** A major bottleneck remains the technical-to-business translation silo. Raw intelligence is often too complex for non-technical stakeholders to act upon in real-time.

### 5. LLMs and Generative AI in the Threat Intelligence Cycle (2025)
*   **Findings:** Large Language Models (LLMs) are being leveraged to refine detection algorithms and provide richer, context-aware intelligence reports. LLMs allow for the automated summarization of dark web forum data, providing quicker attribution to known threat actors.
*   **Limitations:** Over-reliance on generative models can lead to "hallucinations" in attribution. High-volume, AI-generated noise in intelligence feeds requires human-in-the-loop validation to maintain accuracy.

---

### Conclusion
The literature review indicates a clear trajectory: the future of FinTech security lies in **collaborating, AI-driven, and strategic intelligence platforms**. While individual defenses are improving, there is a distinct lack of frameworks that bridge the "Intelligence-to-Enforcement" gap—precisely the void the H3M4 Ecosystem aims to fill. By creating a unified platform for Researchers, Enterprises, and Law Enforcement, we can move beyond isolated defense and towards sector-wide resilience.
